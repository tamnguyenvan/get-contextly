Privacy Policy for Contextly

Last Updated: October 2023

Contextly ("we", "our", or "us") is dedicated to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how our Chrome Extension operates and handles your data.

1. No Data Collection

We do not collect, store, share, or sell any of your personal data, browsing history, or usage analytics. We do not have servers and we do not track you.

2. Data Processing & The Google Gemini API

Contextly acts as a bridge between your browser and the Google Gemini API. When you select text and choose an action (Fix Grammar, Translate, or Summarize):

  • The specific text you selected is sent directly from your browser to Google's servers for processing.
  • Google returns the result to your browser.
  • This data does not pass through any Contextly servers.

Since the extension uses your personal API Key, the processing is subject to Google's Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

3. Local Storage

We use Chrome's local storage API (chrome.storage.local) solely for the following purposes:

  • Storing your Google Gemini API Key.
  • Saving your preferences (e.g., custom tones or target languages).

This data stays on your device and is never synced to us.

4. Permissions

The extension requires permission to access "all websites" purely to detect when you have highlighted text on a page so it can display the floating toolbar. It does not read page content unless you specifically interact with the extension.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. Thus, you are advised to review this page periodically for any changes.
